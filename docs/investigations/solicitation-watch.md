# Solicitation Watch: An Ongoing Investigation

**Series name:** Solicitation Watch  
**Investigating body:** The Institute for Ministry Watch Accountability  
**Lead investigator:** Dr. Cornelius T. Watchwright III, CWO  
**Investigation opened:** June 27, 2026  
**Status:** Active

---

## Investigation Premise

MinistryWatch.com displays a donation solicitation popup immediately upon every homepage visit, regardless of whether the visitor has previously dismissed it. The Institute has determined that an organization whose stated mission is holding Christian ministries accountable to donors has an obligation to model exemplary donor-relations conduct in its own digital presence. This investigation examines whether MinistryWatch's solicitation practices meet the standard it applies to others.

---

## The Popup: Evidence Log

**Observed behavior (as of June 27, 2026):**

- Appears immediately upon homepage load — no scroll trigger, no delay
- Reappears on every subsequent homepage visit, with no memory of prior dismissal
- Contains a close button (X, top right corner), but dismissal is not persisted
- Headline subtext: "BEFORE THE FISCAL YEAR CLOSES"
- Main headline: "Your gift funds the next year of independent reporting."
- Body copy: "MinistryWatch is free, independent, and donor-funded. If MinistryWatch has helped you give wisely, would you consider making a gift?"
- CTA button: "Give before June 30" (dark red/maroon, full-width)
- Footer of popup: "No paywall · No advertising · Donor-supported since 1998"

**Key tensions the Institute has identified:**

1. **The Memory Problem:** The popup has no memory of prior dismissal. Every visit is treated as a first encounter. This raises questions about the technical competence and/or intentional design of the solicitation apparatus.

2. **The Advertising Paradox:** The footer proudly states "No advertising." The popup is, functionally, an advertisement for MinistryWatch itself. The Institute is investigating whether self-promotional popups constitute advertising under a reasonable definition.

3. **The Wisdom Recursion:** MinistryWatch asks "If MinistryWatch has helped you give wisely, would you consider making a gift?" — soliciting funds in the same interface element where it claims to help others give wisely. The Institute finds this philosophically interesting and will continue to examine it.

4. **The Fiscal Year Urgency:** "BEFORE THE FISCAL YEAR CLOSES" implies deadline pressure. The Institute is monitoring whether this language changes after June 30 or whether the fiscal year perpetually closes.

5. **The Donor-Filter Hypothesis:** An emerging theory holds that the persistent popup may be intentional — functioning as a donor-qualification filter. Only visitors willing to tolerate aggressive solicitation would remain on the site long enough to use it, thereby self-selecting for donor temperament. The Institute is neither confirming nor denying this hypothesis at this time, but is watching it closely.

## Threads for Future Installments

- **Does the popup change after June 30?** Monitor whether "Before the Fiscal Year Closes" language persists or rotates. A perpetual fiscal year deadline would itself be a finding.
- **The Advertising Paradox, in depth:** A full accounting of whether a self-promotional popup meets the definition of advertising that MinistryWatch claims not to carry.
- **Comparative analysis:** Do the ministries MinistryWatch rates use similar solicitation tactics? Does MinistryWatch penalize them for it?
- **The Wisdom Recursion:** A theological and epistemological inquiry into whether one can simultaneously help others give wisely and ask them to give to you.
- **Technical findings:** Does the popup use cookies? Local storage? Why is dismissal not persisted? The Institute's Technology Accountability Subcommittee may need to be convened.
- **Reader submissions:** If regular visitors write in with their popup encounter logs, the Institute can begin building a statistical picture of frequency and variation.
