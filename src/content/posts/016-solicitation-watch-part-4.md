**EMERGENCY FILING — Solicitation Watch, Part IV**
*Filed July 1, 2026, 12:47 AM Eastern Time*
*From the Institute's After-Hours Monitoring Station*

---

The fiscal year is over.

The Institute can confirm this because we have a calendar. MinistryWatch told us the fiscal year would end on June 30. June 30 has ended. It is now July 1. The Institute was awake to witness the transition. We will not pretend otherwise.

The popup is still there.

## What The Institute Observed At 12:45 AM

At 12:45 AM Eastern Time on July 1, 2026, a member of the Institute's After-Hours Monitoring Team — a position that did not exist until approximately 11:58 PM on June 30, when it became clear that someone needed to be watching at midnight — navigated to MinistryWatch.com from its apparent operating location in the great state of North Carolina.

The popup appeared.

It was the same popup. Or a popup substantially similar to the one the Institute has been documenting across four installments and what the Institute's legal team describes as "an unreasonable number of billable hours." The apparatus, which has spent the last several weeks insisting that the fiscal year was closing and that gifts were urgently needed before a specific and named deadline, continued to present itself to visitors as though that deadline had not arrived, passed, and been formally archived by the Institute as a closed matter.

The Institute closed it. The popup did not acknowledge this. The Institute is not surprised.

## What The Institute Refuses To Believe

The Institute will now state, for the record, what it refuses to believe.

The Institute refuses to believe that MinistryWatch — an organization whose stated mission is to hold Christian ministries accountable through rigorous, sustained, independent journalism; an organization that watches, that monitors, that reports; an organization that has built its entire institutional identity on the proposition that someone must be paying attention — failed to watch a calendar.

The Institute refuses to believe that the team at MinistryWatch.com, having spent weeks promoting a June 30 fiscal year deadline with a popup, a progress bar, a countdown, and language of escalating urgency, simply did not notice when June 30 ended.

The Institute is aware that this is, in all likelihood, exactly what happened. The Institute is choosing not to engage with this possibility. The Institute has its reasons, and those reasons are that a far more interesting explanation exists.

## The Hostile Interference Hypothesis

At approximately 12:32 AM, having confirmed the popup's continued presence, the Institute convened an emergency session — attendance: two members, one of whom was in pajamas — to consider alternative explanations for the apparatus's post-deadline persistence.

The following hypothesis was developed, debated, and adopted by unanimous vote:

**The MinistryWatch.com homepage has allegedly been compromised by actors hostile to independent evangelical Christian accountability journalism.**

The Institute acknowledges that this is a significant claim. The Institute makes it anyway, because accountability demands it.

Consider the profile of the alleged actors. We are speaking, allegedly, of a government — or a governmental-adjacent entity — whose relationship with donor-funded, independent, Christian journalistic enterprises is not, historically speaking, warm. A government that has, allegedly, taken a dim view of organizations that ask uncomfortable questions about how religious institutions handle money. A government that would find it not merely convenient but, allegedly, ideologically satisfying to leave a fundraising popup running past its announced deadline, thereby undermining MinistryWatch's credibility with its donor base at the precise moment that donor base had been primed for generosity.

The Institute is not naming this government. The Institute is watching the situation. The Institute will say only that the government in question does not, allegedly, share MinistryWatch's commitment to fiscal transparency, and that its methods — allegedly — extend to digital infrastructure.

## The Evidence, Such As It Is

The Institute's evidence for hostile interference is, admittedly, circumstantial. The Institute presents it nonetheless, because circumstantial evidence is still evidence, and the Institute is still a watchdog organization, and watchdogs bark at shadows when that is what accountability requires.

**Exhibit A:** The popup has persisted past its own stated deadline. MinistryWatch is accountable. Accountable organizations do not miss their own deadlines. Therefore, the deadline was not missed by MinistryWatch. Therefore, someone else is responsible for the popup's continued presence. *QED,* allegedly.

**Exhibit B:** The Institute's VIGIL-1 remediation script, filed in Part II and amended in Part III, included the following code — which remains available, free of charge, to MinistryWatch:

```javascript
// Per the popup's own testimony, the fiscal year closes June 30.
// After that date, the urgency — per MinistryWatch's own framing — has passed.
// The Institute takes MinistryWatch at its word. It is only fair.
const FISCAL_YEAR_CLOSE = new Date('2026-06-30T23:59:59');

if (fiscalUrgencyHasPassed()) {
  dismissSolicitationApparatus(apparatus);
  return;
}
```

MinistryWatch did not install this code. Had they done so, the popup would have been automatically suppressed at midnight. That MinistryWatch did not install it suggests one of two things: either MinistryWatch chose not to, or MinistryWatch was prevented from doing so by parties unknown. The Institute is pursuing the latter theory. The former theory is too depressing to file a report on.

**Exhibit C:** It is 12:47 AM. The Institute is awake. Whoever is running the popup is also, allegedly, awake. This is suspicious.

Either way: the popup needs to come down. The fiscal year is over. MinistryWatch said so.
