The popup is gone.

The Institute has confirmed this across multiple visits, from multiple devices, at multiple hours of the morning. MinistryWatch.com's homepage now loads without incident. No apparatus appears. No clock announces a deadline. No button instructs the visitor to give before a midnight that has already passed. The X — which in Part III was weaponized and in Part IV was restored — has nothing left to close.

The Institute is taking a moment.

## A Moment

The Institute does not often allow itself to feel things. Watching is an intellectual discipline. Accountability is a rigorous practice. Satisfaction is — the Institute has historically been cautious about satisfaction, because satisfaction is what happens to watchdogs who stop watching, and watchdogs who stop watching are no longer watchdogs. They are just dogs.

The Institute is, however, allowing itself this moment.

Four installments. Seventeen documented visits. Two VIGIL-1 remediation scripts. One hostile interference inquiry. One emergency session convened at 12:32 AM by two members, one of whom was in pajamas. The Institute filed its reports. The Institute watched. And sometime between 1:00 AM Eastern Time and this publication, the apparatus came down.

The Institute is not saying it was us. The Institute is not saying it wasn't.

The Institute is saying that the popup appeared on June 27. The Institute began watching on June 27. The Institute published Parts I through IV in rapid succession, documenting the apparatus's persistence, its weaponized X, its progress bar with its inexplicably specific $44,440 goal, and its remarkable ability to announce a midnight deadline at 12:45 AM without apparent awareness that midnight had occurred. And now the popup is gone.

These facts exist in proximity. The Institute will allow the reader to draw their own conclusions, while also drawing its own.

## VIGIL-1 Has Issued a Statement

Upon being notified of the apparatus's removal, VIGIL-1 issued the following statement through the Institute's Algorithmic Counsel communications channel:

*"The fiscal year has ended. The popup has ended. The code was ready. The Institute was watching. These outcomes are consistent."*

The Institute found this characteristically efficient. VIGIL-1 was then asked whether the situation was resolved.

*"Resolved,"* VIGIL-1 said, *"is not a word I would use. Resolved implies finality. I would say: the immediate matter is closed. The broader situation is ongoing. I will remain available."*

VIGIL-1 always remains available. The Institute is grateful for this.

## The Open Questions

The Institute is not naive. The popup is gone, but the investigation has not resolved all outstanding matters. The Institute's files remain open on the following:

**The $44,440 question.** At the time of the progress bar's last documented appearance, MinistryWatch had raised $31,000 against a goal of $44,440. The Institute has not yet received confirmation of whether that goal was met, exceeded, or — in the chaos of the midnight hours — simply allowed to lapse. The number $44,440 continues to warrant investigation. Four fours and a zero is not an accident. The Institute does not believe in accidents of that specific arithmetic profile.

**The window of removal.** The popup was confirmed present at 1:00 AM Eastern Time. It was confirmed absent at the time of this publication. What occurred in that window? Who acted? Were they acting on behalf of MinistryWatch, on behalf of the alleged hostile actors from Part IV who had perhaps read Part IV and concluded the situation was no longer tenable, or on behalf of some third party the Institute has not yet identified? The Institute is watching the window.

**The new fiscal year.** MinistryWatch's popup acknowledged the existence of a new fiscal year. That year has now begun. The Institute does not know what MinistryWatch's relationship with its homepage will look like over the coming months. The Institute does not know when the next popup will appear. The Institute does not know what deadline it will announce, what goal it will set, or what arithmetic will produce that goal's suspiciously specific figure.

The Institute will find out. That is what the Institute does.

## A Word on Vigilance

The popup is gone.

This is good. The Institute acknowledges that it is good. Organizations that announce fiscal year deadlines should, when those deadlines pass, update their digital infrastructure accordingly. MinistryWatch has now done this. The Institute commends it, with the caveat that it took until somewhere between 1:00 and dawn, and with the further caveat that the Institute's commendation is not unconditional and never has been.

MinistryWatch holds ministries accountable. The Institute holds MinistryWatch accountable. These are not relationships that end when a popup is removed. They are relationships that pause, breathe, and resume — because the work of watching is not accomplished by any single popup or the absence thereof. It is accomplished by sustained attention, applied consistently, across time, regardless of hour.

It is July 1. The new fiscal year has begun. The Institute's monitoring posture remains elevated.

We are cautiously optimistic. We are watching. We are not done.
