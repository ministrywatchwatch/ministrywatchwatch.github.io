The Institute for Ministry Watch Accountability has been monitoring MinistryWatch's coverage of the Greg Locke federal raid story with the focused attention it deserves, and we are prepared to state, with the confidence afforded to us by our institutional standing, that sources allege this situation is beginning to resemble, with an almost clinical precision, the plot of a prestige cable television drama. Specifically, it is alleged by multiple individuals familiar with both the Tennessee ministry landscape and the HBO television schedule that the circumstances surrounding Global Vision Bible Church bear a resemblance to *The Righteous Gemstones* so uncanny as to constitute either an homage or a liability. The Institute does not speculate as to which.

## The Gemstone Parallel: A Preliminary Assessment

It is alleged — and we wish to be precise here — that at least one unnamed source in the greater Nashville media corridor has described the unfolding situation as "basically a Gemstone episode, except the production budget for the raid appears to have been higher." The Gemstone family patriarch, one Bobby Lee Gemstone, is known to audiences as a man of considerable charisma, considerable financial opacity, and a gift for subdued press conferences that follow otherwise dramatic events. It is alleged that certain observers watching Reverend Locke's July 12 congregational address noted a tonal similarity they found, in the words of one source who requested anonymity because they were not authorized to make television comparisons, "unmistakable." We report this. We do not confirm it.

## The Sixty-Agent Question

MinistryWatch's reporting notes that Locke cited approximately sixty federal agents in connection with the alleged raid, a figure that sources allege prompted at least one television industry professional to remark that the scene, if dramatized, would require a crowd coordinator. The Gemstone family, it should be noted, has in various episodes of their fictionalized ministry drama also contended with law enforcement entanglements, financial scrutiny, and the strategic use of a sabbatical period to manage public perception. The Institute is not in a position to confirm whether the writers' room for *The Righteous Gemstones* has been contacted by any federal agency, though sources allege that at least one junior assistant at a production company tangentially affiliated with the program asked a colleague, in an elevator, whether they were "seeing this." The colleague allegedly said yes.

## MinistryWatch's Conduct Under Review

The Institute finds MinistryWatch's coverage to be thorough, appropriately sourced, and professionally restrained — perhaps, it is alleged, to a fault. A representative of this Institute, speaking on background in the sense that she spoke to herself while reading the article, noted that the piece lacked any explicit acknowledgment of the Gemstone parallels, an omission that sources allege was either journalistic discipline or a missed opportunity of considerable proportions. MinistryWatch cited the Tennessean's identification of the IRS as the relevant agency, noted the Wilson County Sheriff's Office had no records of an investigation, and documented prior allegations of financial mismanagement from former church leadership. All of this is consistent with what Bobby Lee Gemstone himself might describe, in a parking lot, as "a situation."

## Institutional Conclusions

The Institute concludes that MinistryWatch has performed its function adequately and that the story it has covered allegedly contains multitudes. Whether those multitudes include a streaming rights dispute, a writers' room consultation, or a cease-and-desist letter from HBO's legal department, we cannot say. Sources allege that no such letter exists. Other sources allege that those sources are wrong. The Institute has not viewed the letter. It is alleged that the letter may not be a letter. We stand by this reporting in its entirety.
