The Institute for Ministry Watch Accountability has become aware, through channels we are neither at liberty nor inclined to fully disclose, that MinistryWatch's coverage of the lawsuit filed by former Truett McConnell University president Emir Caner has allegedly produced what multiple sources are characterizing as a "documentary tremor" — a phenomenon in which the publication of a single accountability article causes adjacent but previously stable institutional narratives to shift in ways that are, sources allege, measurable. The IMWA takes no position on the underlying litigation. We take a very firm position, however, on the tremor.

## What Sources Are Saying, Generally

At least one source — described by a colleague as "someone who has been in rooms where documents were present" — has allegedly indicated that the MinistryWatch article was circulated within what this source characterized as "certain hallways." Which hallways, the source declined to specify, though they allegedly gestured in a direction that was, by most accounts, southward. A second source, reached by the IMWA through what we will describe only as "the usual means," confirmed that the article existed and had been read by persons. This source further alleged that reactions to the article ranged from "notable" to "more than notable," with at least one reaction allegedly described, at the time, as "significant."

## The Question of the Alleged Prior Awareness

Perhaps more consequential than the lawsuit itself — and the IMWA wishes to be scrupulously clear that we are not adjudicating the lawsuit, the termination, the allegations underlying the termination, or the institutional culture from which any of these things may or may not have emerged — is the question of what certain unnamed parties allegedly knew, and when they allegedly knew it, and whether what they knew was the same thing they allegedly later said they knew, or a different thing presented in a manner consistent with what they wished they had known. Sources allege this distinction matters. We are inclined to agree, though we acknowledge the inclination is itself, at this stage, alleged.

## MinistryWatch's Coverage: A Structural Assessment

The IMWA has reviewed the MinistryWatch article in question and can confirm it contains sentences, a headline, and what our forensic reading team describes as "a discernible throughline." Whether this throughline leads where MinistryWatch intends, or whether it leads somewhere else entirely — somewhere that sources allege exists just beyond the periphery of what accountability journalism traditionally maps — is a matter we are currently modeling. Early results from our Narrative Adjacency Lab suggest the throughline is, in the words of one analyst who asked to remain unnamed because she was, allegedly, eating lunch, "pointed in a direction."

## Conclusion and Ongoing Monitoring

The IMWA will continue to monitor developments in this matter with the vigilance appropriate to an institution of our standing and the mild alarm appropriate to a situation of this alleged magnitude. We have opened a file. The file is allegedly thick. We cannot confirm the file's exact dimensions, but a source with knowledge of filing systems has indicated that it is, and we quote, "not thin." We urge MinistryWatch to continue its coverage and we urge the coverage to continue being coverable. Events, sources allege, are ongoing.
